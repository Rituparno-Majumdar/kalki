# Kalki Code of Conduct

**Kalki: India's Open Intelligence Platform**
**Version**: 1.0
**Effective Date**: May 2026

---

## 1. Our Pledge

We, as contributors, maintainers, and leaders of the Kalki project, pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free, inclusive, and empowering experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

Furthermore, we pledge to act with the highest level of responsibility and civic duty. As a community developing an open-source intelligence and forecasting platform for the Indian public interest, we recognize that data is a powerful instrument. We pledge to use our technical skills, analytical capabilities, and resources exclusively to promote transparency, public welfare, democratic accountability, and community resilience — and never for surveillance, profiling, individual targeting, persecution, or division.

We commit to creating a welcoming and safe environment that respects and celebrates the rich pluralistic tapestry of India's diverse societies.

---

## 2. Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

*   **Respecting India's Pluralism**: Demonstrating deep respect for the vast diversity of India's communities, including across lines of caste, religion, language, gender, region, and tribal heritage.
*   **Responsible Data Handling**: Treating public data with care, maintaining rigorous anonymization, and upholding the aggregation thresholds defined in `ETHICS.md`.
*   **Empathy and Professionalism**: Using welcoming and inclusive language, showing empathy towards other community members, and being supportive of newcomers.
*   **Constructive Collaboration**: Gracefully accepting constructive criticism, focusing on what is best for the collective public interest, and collaborating openly.
*   **Intellectual Honesty**: Documenting data limitations, algorithmic biases, and uncertainties clearly rather than overstating model capabilities.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

*   **Targeting and Discrimination**: The use of project tools, data, or communication spaces to target, harass, or discriminate against individuals or specific communities (especially marginalized demographics, including Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, religious minorites, women, and LGBTQIA+ communities).
*   **Weaponization of Intelligence**: Attempting to implement features, scrapers, or analysis models intended for individual tracking, real-time surveillance, facial recognition, biometric indexing, or predictive policing.
*   **Aggressive Communication**: Troll-like behavior, insulting or derogatory comments, personal or political attacks, and public or private harassment.
*   **Plagiarism and Fraud**: Introducing datasets of questionable provenance, plagiarizing others' code, or fabricating model metrics.
*   **Publishing Private Information**: Publishing or sharing others' private personal information (doxxing), such as physical or electronic addresses, phone numbers, or private credentials.
*   **Unprofessional Conduct**: Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional open-source environment.

---

## 3. Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders (Core Team and Module Maintainers) are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate, fair, and corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and to communicate the reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## 4. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (GitHub repositories, issue trackers, pull requests, wikis, Discord channels, and community calls) and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project or its community in public spaces. Examples of representing our project include using an official email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

---

## 5. Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project's Core Team at `conduct@kalki-intelligence.org` (or the community moderation email designated in `README.md`). All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

---

## 6. Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction
*   **Community Impact**: Use of unprofessional language or other behavior deemed unwelcome in the community.
*   **Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning
*   **Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or a series of actions.
*   **Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions on project spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban
*   **Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained harassing behavior.
*   **Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the project community for a specified period of time (e.g., 1 to 3 months). No professional or informal interaction with the project, including repository contributions or communication, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban
*   **Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, or a single egregious violation (such as using project outputs for surveillance, profiling, or doxxing).
*   **Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction with the project and its repositories. This includes a complete ban from contributing code, writing documentation, joining community chats, or attending community events.

## 8. Kalki-Specific Addendum

As an open-source intelligence platform, Kalki operates under unique ethical responsibilities. The following clauses are legally binding and form an integral part of this Code of Conduct:

### 8.1 Zero-Tolerance Surveillance Policy
Any contributor who uses, modifies, or forks Kalki tools, code, or datasets for the purpose of individual tracking, private surveillance, targeted harassment, state-sponsored or corporate persecution, or discrimination will be **permanently and irreversibly banned** from the Kalki project. The Core Team reserves the right to publish a public notice disclosing the violation and the identity (where known and legally permissible) of the entity violating this policy to protect the community's integrity.

### 8.2 Acknowledgment of Ethical Framework
All contributors working on sensitive analytical modules (including but not limited to `KalkiCrime` and `KalkiDharma`) must explicitly read and acknowledge the `ETHICS.md` and `LEGAL.md` documents. Pull requests modifying analytical models or schema structures will not be merged without a signed declaration (indicated by a checklist item in the pull request template) confirming that the contributor has verified the change complies with k-anonymity, bias-auditing, and aggregate-level constraints.

### 8.3 Public and Data Sovereignty Compliance
All contributors are expected to uphold the data sovereignty guidelines set out in `LEGAL.md`. Code contributions that direct public data processing outside of the Indian subcontinent's sovereign boundaries, or introduce proprietary, third-party software that compromises user audit logs, are strictly prohibited and will be rejected.
